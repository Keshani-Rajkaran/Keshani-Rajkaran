PROJECT 1

ERP Scheduling System ‚Äì McKaylan Motor Engineering

The Business Problem:
McKaylan Motor Engineering relied on paper diaries, phone calls, and in-person visits to manage bookings. Customers had to physically go to the workshop just to request appointments, creating friction, lost opportunities, and limited after-hours enquiries. This manual process caused double-bookings, unrecorded jobs, and serious revenue leakage.

Multi-Role Enterprise Architecture
  • Customer Self-Service Portal: Customers can create accounts, request bookings remotely, and view their confirmed appointments from home. This turned the business into a 24/7 digital storefront and attracted new customers.
  • Admin Operations Console: Admins can create, edit, and delete appointments, manage customer records, and access report history to maintain operational accuracy.
  • Manager Dashboard: Managers generate staff and seasonal performance reports, analyze revenue trends, and assign technicians to jobs for optimized workforce deployment.
  • Staff Management System: Managers can add, edit, and manage technicians and allocate them to specific appointments.
Key Engineering Challenges Solved
  • Role-Based Access Control (RBAC): Secure login layers ensure each user only sees what they are authorized to access.
  • Centralized Relational Database: Prevents double-bookings and maintains consistent records across all subsystems.
  • Business Intelligence Engine: Transforms raw bookings into actionable management insights.
  • UX Architecture: Clean flows between customer booking, admin control, and manager oversight.
Security & Integrity
  • Encrypted authentication & session control
  • Server-side input validation
  • Unauthorized access detection
  • Future-only booking logic protects historical data
Business Impact

The system achieved 100% elimination of double-bookings, enabled remote customer bookings, and gave management real-time control over staff, revenue, and operations.
